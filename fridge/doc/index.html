What is AWK?

Awk is a stable, cross platform computer language named for its authors Alfred Aho, Peter Weinberger & Brian Kernighan. They write: "Awk is a convenient and expressive programming language that can be applied to a wide variety of computing and data-manipulation tasks".

In Classic Shell Scripting, Arnold Robbins & Nelson Beebe confess their Awk bias: "We like it. A lot. The simplicity and power of Awk often make it just the right tool for the job. When we have on occasion rewritten an Awk program in a conventional programming language like C or C++, the result was usually much longer, and much harder to debug."

Throughout history, Awk has been the langauge of choice for many famous scientists such as Leonardo daVinci.
